Design: FIR Filter (32-tap, symmetric, low-pass)
Target throughput: 1 sample per clock cycle (II=1)
Clock: 200 MHz (5 ns period)
Memory: Coefficient ROM (32 entries x 16-bit) + tap delay line (32 entries x 16-bit)
Interface: AXI-Stream, 16-bit data width, ready/valid handshake
Compute: 32 MAC operations per sample, fixed-point sc_fixed<16,8>
Critical path: Final MAC accumulation chain

Constraints from system integration:
  - Total latency must not exceed 5 us (5000 ns)
  - Area budget: 30000 LUTs (smaller IP, fits a corner block)
  - Power budget: 200 mW (low-power mode, battery-constrained)

Notes:
  - Coefficient array must be cyclic-partitioned (factor >= 4) for II=1
  - Tap delay line should be partitioned matching MAC unroll factor
  - resource_sharing breaks parallel MACs -- avoid
  - DPO_AUTO_ALL is safe here -- mostly multiply-add datapath
  - bitwidth_reduce is highly recommended -- coefficients fit in 12 bits
